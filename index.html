About Me

Hi, I’m Zurab. I’m currently a Big Data Engineer at the Bank of Georgia, working in the Customer Tribe’s Offers Hub. Alongside developers and business teams, I help internal teams create and deliver personalized offers, as well as external partner products, directly to customers. On a daily basis, I work with Spark, Kafka, Airflow, Yarn, and Hadoop, designing, monitoring, and maintaining both batch and streaming data pipelines to ensure campaigns run reliably, efficiently, and on time.

My journey started with a love for science and problem-solving. As a school student, I competed in international Math and Physics Olympiads as a member of the Georgian National Team. Winning a Silver Medal at the International Physics Olympiad 2018 in Lisbon—a first for my country—earned me a full scholarship to New York University, where I studied Mathematics and Machine Learning.

Driven by curiosity in quantum science, I continued my academic path at the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics, one of the world’s top five physics research centers, completing an MSc in Quantum Physics and exploring the foundations of quantum science in depth. Today, I combine this scientific rigor with engineering expertise to build scalable, high-performance data systems that make a tangible impact.

I have broad interests in data/AI technologies, quantum science, and quantitative finance, and I plan to publicly share various projects along the way.
